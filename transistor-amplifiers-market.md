<p><p>Transistor amplifiers are electronic devices that amplify and increase the strength of electrical signals. There are three primary types based on their operating characteristics â€“ Class-A, Class-B, and Class-AB. Class-A amplifiers deliver a continuous current flow through the transistor, providing highest linearity but with low efficiency. Class-B amplifiers only conduct current during half of the input wave cycle, offering higher efficiency but with reduced linearity. Class-AB amplifiers combine the characteristics of Class-A and Class-B, providing better efficiency and linearity than Class-A but lower than Class-B.</p></p>
