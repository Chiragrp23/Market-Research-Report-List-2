<p><p>Electronic tubes, also known as vacuum tubes, are electronic devices used for amplifying and controlling electrical signals. They are categorized into three main types based on their operating characteristics: Class-A, Class-B, and Class-AB.</p><p>Class-A tubes operate in a state where the input signal is always present and the output signal replicates the entire input waveform. These tubes are known for their low distortion but are less efficient and produce more heat.</p><p>Class-B tubes operate by amplifying only half of the input signal waveform. They offer higher efficiency but can introduce more distortion due to the cutoff of one half of the waveform.</p><p>Class-AB tubes combine the characteristics of Class-A and Class-B. They operate with both halves of the input waveform, providing a compromise between efficiency and distortion. These tubes are commonly used in audio amplifiers to achieve a balance between power output and sound quality.</p></p>
