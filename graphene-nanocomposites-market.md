<p><p>Graphene nanocomposites are advanced materials made by combining graphene with other substances, resulting in improved properties and functionalities. There are three main types of graphene nanocomposites: Graphene Oxide (GO), Graphene Nano Platelets (GNP), and Graphene Polymer. GO is produced from graphite, chemically modified to contain oxygen functionalities. GNP consists of thin, flat sheets of graphene stacked together. Graphene Polymer involves mixing graphene with polymers to enhance their mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties. These nanocomposites have numerous applications in industries such as electronics, aerospace, and energy storage.</p></p>
