<p><p>Barrier packaging materials are used to protect products from external factors like moisture, oxygen, and light. PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) provides excellent barrier properties, making it suitable for packaging carbonated drinks and food products. CPP (Cast Polypropylene) offers high moisture resistance, making it ideal for perishable food packaging. BOPP (Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene) provides good barrier properties against oxygen and moisture, commonly used for snacks and confectionery packaging. PVA (Polyvinyl Alcohol) is especially effective against water vapor and is utilized in pharmaceutical and cosmetic packaging. PLA (Polylactic Acid), a biodegradable material derived from renewable resources, is popular for sustainable packaging choices. Other barrier packaging materials include materials like aluminum foil and metallized films, offering exceptional barrier properties for packaging sensitive products.</p></p>
